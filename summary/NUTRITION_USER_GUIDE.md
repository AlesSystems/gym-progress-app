# Nutrition Tracking - User Guide

## Accessing Nutrition Tracking

From the Dashboard, tap the **üçé Nutrition** button in the quick actions section.

## Setting Up Your Goals

1. Open the Nutrition screen
2. Tap the **üéØ Goals** icon in the top-right corner
3. Enter your daily calorie goal (e.g., 2500)
4. Enter your daily protein goal in grams (e.g., 150)
5. Tap **Save**

## Logging Today's Nutrition

### Quick Log
1. From the Nutrition screen, tap **Log** button in the "Today" card
2. Enter your total calories consumed
3. Enter your total protein consumed (in grams)
4. Optionally add notes about your meals
5. Tap **Save**

### Edit Existing Entry
If you've already logged today's nutrition:
1. Tap **Edit** button in the "Today" card
2. Update the values
3. Tap **Save**

## Viewing Progress

### Today Card
- Shows your current day's logged calories and protein
- Displays progress percentage toward your daily goals
- Green percentage indicates you're on track

### Last 7 Days
- View your weekly average calories and protein intake
- See individual entries for each of the past 7 days
- Days without entries show "Not logged"

## Logging Past Days

1. Scroll to the "Last 7 Days" section
2. Tap on any day you want to log
3. Enter the nutrition data for that specific day
4. Tap **Save**

## Tips

- **Daily consistency**: Try to log your nutrition at the end of each day
- **Protein priority**: The app focuses on calories and protein, the two most important metrics for strength training
- **Simple tracking**: You don't need to track every macro‚Äîjust calories and protein
- **Notes field**: Use notes to record special meals or important observations

## Philosophy

Nutrition tracking in this app is designed to be **simple and focused**:
- Track only what matters: calories and protein
- No meal planning complexity
- No food databases to search through
- Quick daily logging, not obsessive tracking
- Complements your strength training goals

## Integration with Workouts

While nutrition and workout data are tracked separately, you can:
- View both your workout and nutrition progress
- Correlate strength gains with nutrition habits
- Make informed decisions about your diet and training
